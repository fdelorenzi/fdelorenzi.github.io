My relationship with software and cybersecurity started in 2005 when I built my first malware removal tool at age twelve. It was downloaded over three hundred thousand times and covered by BBC News, RAIUNO, ANSA, and Panorama. That early experience shaped how I think about building things: solve real problems, ship them, and take security seriously from day one.

Twenty years later I’m still doing exactly that — only the surface area has grown.

What I do now

  • I run LeaderNova, a tech-leadership and product studio based in Puglia, Italy. We serve clients across 8+ countries, embedding as outcome owners rather than vendors. Our work spans fractional CTO leadership, dedicated product teams, AI and business automation, custom SaaS development, and vibe-code security audits.
  • I support Vinted as Product Engineer, contributing to high-performance marketplace platform development at scale.
  • I serve as Vice President at PugliaTechs, a nonprofit working to transform Puglia into a global tech hub by connecting startups, investors, institutions, and professionals. Co-organising PugliaHack 2026.
  • I work as a computer forensics consultant for law firms and law enforcement in criminal and civil proceedings, and contribute open-source tools for forensic evidence acquisition and analysis.

Areas of focus

  • Technical Leadership — Fractional CTO engagements, CTO mentorship, delivery system design, roadmap alignment, and engineering culture.
  • Product & Engineering — End-to-end product development for SaaS, marketplace, and automation platforms. Modern stack: Next.js, NestJS, FastAPI, Supabase, Vercel.
  • Cybersecurity — DevSecOps, web application security, penetration testing, vibe-code security audits for AI-generated codebases, OWASP Top 10 and OWASP Top 10 for LLMs.
  • AI & Automation — Business process automation, AI agent development, systems integration, n8n workflow orchestration.
  • Digital Forensics — Mobile and web evidence acquisition, OSINT/SOCMINT, expert witness in criminal and civil proceedings.
